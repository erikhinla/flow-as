# Context Architecture Assessment
Authority: BIZBUILDERS/systems/intake/
Canon reference: CANONICAL_SOURCE_OF_TRUTH.md Section 10

## Purpose

This assessment maps where digital friction is blocking momentum in your business.
It is not a quiz. It is not a score. It produces a baseline — an honest picture of
where your current state is stable and where it is not.

Completion time: 10 to 15 minutes.
What you get: an observable baseline and a clear routing decision.

---

## Section 1 — Context Layer (TransformBy10X)

These questions assess whether you have a stable source of truth for how your
business operates.

**1.1 — Source of Truth**
Do you have one place where your business logic, offer structure, and positioning
are documented and consistently referenced?

- [ ] Yes — it is documented, current, and used
- [ ] Partially — it exists but is outdated or inconsistently used
- [ ] No — it exists in my head or across scattered notes
- [ ] No — I have not built this yet

**1.2 — Terminology Consistency**
When you or your team describe what you do, is the language consistent across
channels (website, sales calls, emails, onboarding)?

- [ ] Yes — consistent across all surfaces
- [ ] Mostly — minor inconsistencies
- [ ] No — it shifts depending on who is communicating or what the context is

**1.3 — Decision-Making**
When a new opportunity or problem arises, do you have a defined framework for
deciding what to pursue?

- [ ] Yes — I have a documented decision process
- [ ] Informally — I have mental rules but nothing written
- [ ] No — decisions happen case by case without a framework

## Section 2 — System Architecture Layer (BizBuilders)

These questions assess whether your operational infrastructure is built and stable.

**2.1 — Workflow Documentation**
Are your core business workflows (client intake, delivery, follow-up, reporting)
documented and repeatable?

- [ ] Yes — documented, followed, and updated when they change
- [ ] Partially — some workflows are documented, others are not
- [ ] No — workflows exist in practice but are not written down
- [ ] No — workflows are inconsistent or underdefined

**2.2 — Execution Paths**
When a client moves through your system, is the path from first contact to
delivered outcome clearly defined?

- [ ] Yes — every step is defined and owned
- [ ] Partially — the path exists but has gaps or handoff failures
- [ ] No — the path is unclear or varies significantly by client

**2.3 — Tooling and Configuration**
Are the tools you use to run your business configured for your specific workflows,
or are they used as defaults with manual workarounds?

- [ ] Configured — tools are set up to match our workflows
- [ ] Partially configured — some tools are customized, others are default
- [ ] Default — tools are used out of the box with significant manual work
- [ ] Inconsistent — different team members use tools differently

**2.4 — Automation Coverage**
What percentage of your repeatable operational tasks are automated?

- [ ] More than 60%
- [ ] 30 to 60%
- [ ] Less than 30%
- [ ] Essentially none

## Section 3 — Activation Layer (BizBot)

These questions assess whether your growth and lead systems are operational.

**3.1 — Lead Capture**
Do you have a defined and functioning system for capturing leads?

- [ ] Yes — functional, tested, producing leads
- [ ] Partially — a system exists but is not consistently used or optimized
- [ ] No — leads come in ad hoc with no defined capture system

**3.2 — Follow-Up**
Is your follow-up process documented and executed consistently?

- [ ] Yes — automated or consistently manual with defined steps
- [ ] Partially — follow-up happens but varies by rep or situation
- [ ] No — follow-up is reactive and inconsistent

**3.3 — Content and Outreach**
Do you have a defined system for producing and distributing content or outreach?

- [ ] Yes — a calendar, workflow, and distribution system exists
- [ ] Partially — content is produced but without a consistent system
- [ ] No — content happens when time allows

## Section 4 — Interface Layer (Scale and Access)

These questions assess whether your client-facing interfaces match your
underlying system.

**4.1 — Client-Facing Accuracy**
Does your website, onboarding, and sales materials accurately reflect how your
system actually works?

- [ ] Yes — fully aligned
- [ ] Mostly — minor gaps between what is promised and what is delivered
- [ ] No — significant gaps between what clients expect and what they receive

**4.2 — Intake Experience**
When a new client enters your system, is the experience consistent and governed
by a defined process?

- [ ] Yes — consistent, documented, and tested
- [ ] Partially — a process exists but varies
- [ ] No — intake is handled ad hoc
